I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Helmholtz Institute for Human-Centered AI, working with Dr. Eric Schulz.
My research is focused on testing computational models of cognition.

Computational cognitive modeling

My research centers on developing computational cognitive models that translate cognitive theories into concise algorithms with a limited number of parameters that map onto cognitive mechanisms impacting observable behavior. This work involves quantifying individual differences in information processing through model parameters, pinpointing the most suitable theories embedded within various cognitive models, and investigating the interactions among cognitive processes like executive function, working memory, and reinforcement learning.

Model fitting tools

Cognitive science researchers heavily rely on tools to connect computational cognitive models with data, often through likelihood estimation. However, numerous models do not have tractable likelihood functions, thus limiting the range of testable theories. My work has also focused on implementing an alternative model-fitting approach using simple, light-weight artificial neural networks that enables parameter estimation and model identification solely based on data sequences, while circumventing the need for likelihood estimation.

Published work:

Rmus, M., Pan, T., Xia, L. & Collins, A. G. E. (2024). Artificial neural networks for model identification and parameter estimation in computational cognitive models. PLOS Computational Biology PDF
Rmus, M., Xia, J., Collins, J. & Collins, A. G. E. (2022). Using Deep Learning tools for fitting Reinforcement Learning Models. Conference on Computational Cognitive Neuroscience Proceedings. PDF

Hierarchical reinforcement learning

Hierarchical reinforcement learning presents an effective solution to the challenges of generalization and scalability that classical reinforcement learning models often encounter. Within the HRL framework, intricate learning tasks are broken down into sub-components through the use of pseudo-reinforcing subgoals, which can be applied to new tasks. My research centers on investigating the process by which individuals discover these subgoals.

Published work:

Rmus, M., Eckstein, M., & Collins, A. G. E. (2023). The role of subgoals in hierarchical reinforcement learning. Conference on Computational Cognitive Neuroscience Proceedings. PDF
